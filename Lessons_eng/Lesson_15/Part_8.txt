Certain prefixes (co-, pre-, mid-, de-, non-, anti-, etc.) may or may not be hyphenated. Hyphens are occasionally used to denote syllabification, as in syl-la-bi-fi-ca-tion.
The at sign (@), also called the at symbol or asperand, is formally an abbreviation of the accounting and commercial invoice term "at the rate of" (e.g., 9 widgets @ $3 = $27).
